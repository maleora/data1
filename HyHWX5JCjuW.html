More About Sleep Till Noon

My first Max Shulman readVery humorous. I can see how the author had influenced comedians of the sixties and seventies. The book still stands up today. Typical Shulman zaniness. The problem is that without Dobie Gillis, much of the inanity comes off as a bit shrill at times and downright vicious at others. Going to stick to Dobie from now on. This book has a sense of humor that you don't find in books today (it was published in 1950). I haven't read Max Shulman before, but I'd like to find another of his books and check him out again.
